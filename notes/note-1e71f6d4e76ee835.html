🇪🇺 Corrigendum deletes the sub-193 nm light-source criterion from Annex I entry 3B501.f.1.a

📅 Published 14 July 2026 · high confidence EU 500-series

What changed

  • The corrigendum of 9 February 2026 to Delegated Regulation (EU) 2025/2003 replaces the text of entry 3B501.f.1.a, 'A light source wavelength shorter than 193 nm; or', with 'Not used;' [OJ L 2026/90092, entry 3B501.f.1.a].
  • 'Not used' is the Annex I placeholder for a deleted provision: the sub-193 nm light-source criterion no longer exists as a route into entry 3B501.f.1, whose remaining sub-points are unchanged [OJ L 2026/90092, entry 3B501.f.1.a].

Who is affected — Exporters and holders of prior classifications of align-and-expose step-and-repeat or step-and-scan wafer-processing equipment assessed under entry 3B501.f.1 [CELEX 02021R0821-20251115, entry 3B501.f.1].

Licence posture — not stated in source

Effective — Correction applies as part of Regulation (EU) 2025/2003, in force since 15 November 2025 — 'the day following that of its publication in the Official Journal' [CELEX 32025R2003, Art. 2]

Instruments — Annex I, Regulation (EU) 2021/821 — entry 3B501.f.1.a

Relevance for EU exporters — Directly applicable: Annex I of Regulation (EU) 2021/821 governs EU exporters' classification obligations, and the corrected criterion gates entry 3B501.f.1 [OJ L 2026/90092, entry 3B501.f.1.a].

Annex I entries affected — 3B501

Δ 3B501.f.1.a: “A light source wavelength shorter than 193 nm; or” → “Not used;”

Reclassification impact — Holders of prior classifications relying on entry 3B501.f.1.a should revalidate against the corrected entry text: the sub-193 nm light-source criterion no longer exists, and equipment caught solely by it falls outside 3B501.f.1.a.

Action items

  • Revalidate any classification that relied on the deleted sub-193 nm light-source criterion of entry 3B501.f.1.a — including lithography equipment previously caught by it.
  • Record the Annex I version relied upon (02021R0821-20251115) in the classification register.
